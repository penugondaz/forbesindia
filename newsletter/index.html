Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

US President Donald Trump kept up his streak of keeping weekends busy with his comments on Indian immigrants to the country and the fourth attempt on his life.

Any US president probably has the world’s highest security brief – one which hasn’t protected Trump from being the target of assassination attempts multiple times over. The one on Saturday was carried out by a 31-year-old at the White House Correspondents’ Association dinner in Washington DC, where several high-profile diplomats and journalists were present.

To Trump’s credit, he came across brave and unshaken after the security breach. He also said that the attempt portrayed the power his presidency holds.

However, his posts on Truth Social citing a podcast ruffled some feathers in Delhi. The US President posted the transcript of remarks by podcaster Michael Savage on the “abuse of privilege” of America’s birthright citizenship by Indians and Chinese.

The transcript said, “A baby here becomes an instant citizen, and then they bring the entire family in from China or India or some other hellhole on the planet,” inviting criticism from the Indian political community.

Unrelated incidents, showing the contrast in the US President’s perception among the citizens and what keeps him in power.

📱Tim Cook’s report card from Apple

Tim Cook’s elevation to the role of executive chairman of the company and ushering in John Ternus as the new CEO marks the end of an era for Apple. Cook brought the consumer electronics company out of his predecessor Steve Jobs’ shadows aiming for new developments and markets.

➤Cook’s tenure has been marked by the company’s foray into wearables like the Apple Watch, services focus and its transition to using its own chips for the devices.

➤Cook has also been known to have managed to maintain cordial relations with the White House under the current administration.

➤However, Cook’s 15-year tenure hasn’t seen a category defining innovation or made much progress in AI enabled features.

📈Global and domestic factors challenge India’s growth story

The double whammy from global unrest and threat of rising inflation has dulled the sheen on India’s growth story for global brokerage firms. The recent open letter by brokerage firm Bernstein addressed to the PM warns of structural weaknesses in India.

➤In the letter, Bernstein refers to India trailing peers on physical infra, innovation capacity and preparedness for the next wave of tech innovation.

➤The weak guidance by India’s IT majors indicates weak demand and margin pressures.

➤The letter also points at India remaining a mere consumer in the AI economy without a control on frontier technology.

💊Coming soon, weight loss pills in India

In the race to capture semaglutide-led weight loss market, Chinese pharma companies are doubling down on developing pills given the consumer preference for oral medication over injectables. This could soon disrupt the India market too, paving the way for increased India-China licensing deals in the segment.

➤Nearly a dozen oral GLP-1 molecules are under various stage of development cross Chinese pharma companies.

➤In India, the oral GLP-1 drugs currently available are from Novo Nordisk and Torrent pharma. Dr Reddy’s recently received an approval for its oral formulation.

➤Doctors believe oral GLP-1s could improve adoption and increase long-term adherence.
