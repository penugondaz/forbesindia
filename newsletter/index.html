Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

The World Cups have begun – yes, both the FIFA World Cup 2026 and ICC Women’s T20 World Cup 2026. Indians make up for an active viewer base for both, though the scale of the sports is incomparable.Indians will make up for nearly 300 million viewing audience for the FIFA World Cup according to KPMG, compared to 446 million digital audience for the Women’s T20 World Cup in tournament-wide reach in 2025.

This year promises to be bigger than before, given the latest announcement on the use of immersive broadcast technology, high-quality camera production and mobile-first live coverage promised by the International Cricket Council (ICC) for the tournament.

That said, an estimated 20,000 Indians will probably be able to watch FIFA matches to be held across Canada, US and Mexico in person, compared to the previous event of 2022 held in Qatar which saw nearly 56,000 Indians turn up. Strict visa scrutiny despite the US dropping the clause of $15,000 refundable security deposit for B1/B2 visa applicants from high-risk countries and inflated ticket prices are squarely to blame.

In the list of what FIFA got wrong about fans, other than inflating ticket-prices to comparable American sports, its variable pricing strategy, and an inventory of 180,000 unsold tickets two days ahead of the tournament, is the pricing for broadcast rights.

In India Zee Entertainment scooped up the exclusive broadcast and streaming rights for the tournament in the last minute for a reported deal of $40 million. This is way less than the asking price of nearly $100 million from FIFA, and lower than the 2022 deal of $60 million paid by Viacom18.

If you are among the lucky few, you have earned bragging rights for your $8000 or higher tickets for the football World Cup finals in New Jersey on July 19. For the rest of football-crazy Indians, streaming will have to do this time around. The grapes aren’t sour; it’s just not the season.

📉IPO-bound tech companies pray for a stable market

The IPO-boom for India’s new age tech companies has hit a roadblock due to weak market sentiment, valuation reset, and flagging interest from retail investors. After a blockbuster year in 2025 when these companies accounted for nearly a fourth of overall IPO fundraising, there has been a significant slowdown thus far in 2026 since the month of March. However, the story is far from over believe experts as global uncertainties play out to a conclusion.

🏏Indian cricket’s teen sensation, Vaibhav Sooryavanshi

Teenage cricket player Vaibhav Sooryavanshi’s star is on the rise on-field and off it. After breaking IPL records in his second outing in the tournament with 72 sixes and 776 runs, Sooryavanshi is notching big brand deals.

➤Since his breakout IPL season, Sooryavanshi’s fee for brand endorsements has more than doubled to Rs 2 crore and upwards.

➤Apart from natural brand fits like health drinks, Sooryavanshi’s on-field pyrotechnics have also been able to connect with the 12-16 age group who aren’t necessarily cricket fans, opening new avenues for brand deals.

➤With age on his side, the young cricketer has multiple seasons to recover any bad seasons he might have in the future and grow with brand deals in the long-term.
