Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

The workers’ protest in Noida and other parts of the National Capital Region have come to a head.

What started as a protest by factory workers in Noida saw domestic workers joining in, demanding a hike in wages to counter inflationary pressures and demanding better working conditions. A high-level committee appointed by the Uttar Pradesh government has recommended a 21 percent increase in wages across 74 scheduled employments, applicable from April 1.

Nearly 203 contractors violating the labour laws have been arrested by the government, said news outlets. Interestingly, an engineer, Aditya Anand, labelled the “mastermind” behind the violence and arson by the protestors on April 13, has been arrested by the Noida police, according to reports.

For now, the protestors have been assuaged, but factories will be grappling with wage pressure and labour crisis for some time to come.

For now, they have managed to clock a fair day’s wage for a fair day’s work.

💊Weight loss’s magic pill moment

The expiry on Novo Nordisk’s patent on semaglutides in India has opened the $1 billion domestic sales market to generic weight loss and diabetes drugs. Globally, the opportunity is even bigger, with the market for GLP-1 based drugs exceeding $100 billion to $150 billion annually over the decade. While great news for those in need, the falling prices and easy availability also comes with the risk of misuse.

📱The rising cost of smartphones

India’s smartphone market is facing a double-whammy – first the ongoing chip shortage due to diversion of resources to support AI workloads, and now, the West-Asia crisis. This has had an impact on smartphone prices in the country, with the cost of entry-level devices going up to Rs 20,000.

➤Shipment of smartphones witnessed a 9 percent year-on-year decline.

➤The cost of NAND and DRAM memory chips has gone up by nearly 50 percent.

➤The market share of sub-15k phones has gone down in January to March quarter 2026 due to hike in memory chips

🥚India’s consumer firms brace for war impact

The West Asia crisis is going to have long-term impact on the Indian consumer story as well, with earnings growth beginning to slow down. The impact is likely to be more pronounced in Q1 2027 due to tightening margins, volatile export markets, and a weak rural demand.

➤Growth is being driven by urban and premium segments, as rural and mass demand remains weak.

➤FMCG firms are likely to cut grammage or raise prices, with mass market being vulnerable to this.

➤Companies with strong food portfolios are likely to perform better with stable demand and better pricing power.

☔Below average monsoons might push inflation higher

The weak monsoon predictions are adding to the overall squeeze in the market. The recent forecast by IMD predicting the weakest monsoon in 26 years is likely to further weaken rural demand, increasing inflation. Companies in the FMCG, consumer durables, and auto space will likely bear the brunt due to pressured farm income.

➤The IMD predicted the June to September rain at 92 percent of the average, lower than normal.

➤In addition to rising agriculture input costs, this is likely to accentuate food inflation.

➤Agriculture and allied activities employ 46 percent of the workforce and supports more than half the population in India.
