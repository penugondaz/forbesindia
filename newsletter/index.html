Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

In a season which started with big money flowing in from private equity, the sheen around Indian Premier League is losing some of its sparkle.The start was promising with Blackstone-backed consortium placing its bet on Royal Challengers Bangalore, to the finale on Sunday, with the reigning champions taking home the title. The same can't be said of the fandom's engagement with the league.

IPL matches saw an 18.8 percent drop in TV ratings and a 26 percent in average viewership, according to data from BARC India. However, digital viewership driven by ubiquitous smartphone viewing has continued to expand.

Experts blame this on changing consumer habits as viewers prefer watching short clips, highlights or reels of the match rather than the entire match itself.

And the other key factor impacting IPL viewership has been the ban on Real Money Gaming (RMG). The ban has seen the viewers walk away from an immersive viewing experience where each ball mattered, due to skin-in-the-game. The ban has also wiped off nearly Rs 1500 crore in ad spends by the RMG companies during IPL.

The final nail in the coffin last week was Supreme Court’s ruling which mandates RMG platforms to pay 28 percent GST on the entire amount staked by players, including retrospective tax notices sent to these firms.

However, reports claim that betting on IPL continues, albeit on the fringes.

🤖Limited AI exposure proves to be an impediment in India’s m-cap growth

This week Taiwan nudged ahead of India to claim the spot of the fifth most valued capital market globally, right after US, China, Japan, and Hong Kong. As India’s benchmark indices Sensex and Nifty fell 8 to 10 percent during the year, Taiwan’s Taiex has seen a super rally rising by almost 50 percent this year.

The rally in Taiex is driven by semiconductors and AI-linked stocks, with the Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company gaining nearly 130 percent during the year.

AI driven stocks will continue to remain a key driver in the second half of 2026 as well, strengthening markets in North Asia. South Asia dominated by old-economy sectors will have few markets standing strong.

Experts believe that continued FII outflows from India have led to poorer relative returns, though recovery is in sight.

📡NCDEX lets you hedge for monsoon fluctuations

Indian agricultural commodity exchange National Commodity & Derivatives Exchange (NCDEX) has launched the country’s first exchange-traded weather derivative contract – RAINMUMBAI. This offers a one-of-its-kind market tool to manage monsoon linked risks in India, even as the IMD predicted below-normal rainfall this year.

RAINMUMBAI is based on rainfall data in Mumbai, based on rainfall deviations from Mumbai’s Long Period Average (LPA) from June to September.

The cash-settled futures contract which opened for trading on May 29, will be traded in 1 mm (millimeter), with a lot-multiplier of Rs 50 per mm and a maximum order size of 50 lots.

The product has been designed for sectors like agriculture, power and utilities, infrastructure, and construction, affected by variable rainfall.
