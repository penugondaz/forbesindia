It’s time – semaglutides are now cheaper than monthly gym membership in India!

Indian generic drug makers like Dr Reddy’s, Glenmark, Sun Pharma, Zydus Lifesciences, and Natco Pharma have launched their own brand of semaglutides at half the price of branded drugs such as Ozempic and Wegovy.

Mankind, Cipla, Biocon and Alkem are all in the last leg of launching their own brands in this space.

For the uninitiated, GLP -1 (glucagon-like peptide) is a human hormone regulating blood sugar and appetite. Semaglutides are a class of drugs called GLP-1 receptor agonists which mimic the hormone to slow down digestion, stimulate insulin production and are used to treat Type-2 diabetes and obesity.

On Friday March 20th, the patent for semaglutides in India expired – meaning generic drug makers could launch their own version of the drugs in the market restricted to innovators like Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly.

This makes the drug accessible to all classes of Indians, not just celebrities who hit the headlines for their “miraculous” weight-loss journeys. It also opens a can of worms on controlling counterfeit, misuse of the off-prescription drugs and surrogate advertising pushing drugs to manage obesity over long-term lifestyle changes.

It would be interesting to see how much regulation truly works to let Indian generic makers grow and corner a piece of the lucrative market in the country and abroad. After all, India’s generics export is expected to reach $130 billion in total value by 2030 and currently serves 20 percent of the global generics export by volume.

🌆Indian investments in Dubai slow down

Indians love a good real-estate deal, especially if it comes with tax-free yields and a Golden Visa. This has been a major draw for HNIs from India to invest in UAE’s growth story across residential and office properties. However, the ongoing war has dulled the sheen as missiles targeted Dubai’s financial district and airport.

☑️Indians are the single largest cohort of buyers in Dubai’s housing market, making up a quarter of the market.

☑️Indian housing purchases in Dubai jumped five-fold from 2021 to 2025 in terms of value.

☑️Indian celebrities and sports personalities own branded residences in Dubai including Shah Rukh Khan, Salman Khan, the Bachchans, as well as Alia Bhatt and Ranbir Kapoor.

🌱War escalates fertiliser prices

The impact of war continues to rattle the Indian middle- and lower-income groups, this time through potential rise in food prices. The shortage of LPG and increasing price of inputs has led to reduction in domestic Urea production. This might lead to increasing cost of the inputs for India’s farmers.

☑️India is among the world’s largest fertiliser consumers, dependent on imports for finished compounds and intermediates.

☑️The fertiliser industry in India accounts for nearly 30 percent of natural gas consumption.

☑️Due to the erratic supply of natural gas, fertiliser manufacturers are competing for it against gas meant for domestic consumption.

🛫Government clears the air for better travel experience

No more fighting for the armrest in flights if you are unwilling to shell out extra bucks for the seat. The Ministry of Civil Aviation announced “passenger-focused measures” last week to improve transparency in air travel and improve the overall experience.

☑️Airlines across the board have raised fares and introduced fuel surcharges following the rise in fuel prices due to the war.

☑️As part of the measures announced, airlines will have to allocate atleast 60 percent of the seats for free.

☑️The DGCA has also iterated that the airlines will have to follow passenger rights rules for delays, cancellations and denied boarding.
