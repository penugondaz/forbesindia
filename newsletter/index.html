Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

Meta's instant messaging and calling app WhatsApp, appointed Kunal Shah as its CEO last week. The appointment stands out as Shah was scouted by the company for the role and is the first Indian CEO of Meta group companies.

He has also stepped away from financial services venture CRED, in which Meta has invested $900 million for a 20 percent stake.

His rise to the role is well-chronicled; however, the appointment by Meta is a strategic move to appoint someone who understands WhatsApp’s largest user base by region.

WhatsApp has an estimated 500 million-plus Monthly Active Users in India as of 2025, the number of users for WhatsApp Business in India stood at 15 million as of 2020, post which Meta hasn’t commented on the numbers. As per research sites, the MAU in India currently is over 850 million!

While Meta has maintained that it will not be monetising private chats, it has come up with various strategies to monetise business messaging through ads in Updates tab, paid channels, subscriptions among others. Its recent announcement for India on Business AI supporting all Indian languages also mentioned UPI-based ecommerce in business conversations between businesses and customers. The strategy requires a fintech veteran to manage relations with NPCI, ensure tech implementation, and manage the risk of fraud.

Shah fits the bill, having built payments app FreeCharge and later CRED. To be fair, Meta did attempt to appoint Indian talent earlier to get WhatsApp payments off the ground, the customer-facing feature received an approval too late in the day to have a significant impact.

Apart from monetisation, his success as the CEO depends on steering WhatsApp towards its global super-app ambitions, while navigating global pressure on transparency and traceability of messages.

📉Deceleration in petro-based production drags down core sector growth in May

India’s index of eight core industries (ICI) recorded the slowest growth in seven months in May. The core sector index grew only 0.5 percent year-on-year in May 2026, as compared to a growth of 1.2 percent in May 2025. The deceleration is primarily attributed to reduced production by petro-based industries, due to fallout from global supply and import dynamics.

➤The sharpest drag came from coal which contracted 9.3 percent, registering negative growth for the third month in a row.

➤Output of petroleum refinery products fell by 8.7 percent, while crude oil output fell by 4.6 percent and natural gas contracted by 4.9 percent.

➤Output across electricity, cement and steel managed strong growth, despite the pressure.

🔄India can’t connect to satellite internet, yet

Despite partnerships in place, Indian consumers cannot access satellite broadband services at scale as spectrum rules, security clearances, pricing mechanisms, and regulatory frameworks take time to catch up.

➤Last week, the government released the Authorisation Rules 2026 to replace India’s licensing regime for telecom services with an authorisation-based system.

➤As part of the rules, the government has removed the proposed 20 percent local sourcing requirement for satellite internet operators.

➤Despite the agreements between Indian telcos and satellite internet providers like SpaceX, Eutelsat OneWeb, and Amazon Leo, India is six to 12 months away from meaningful adoption due to final regulatory clearances.
