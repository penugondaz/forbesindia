Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

The Prime Minister on Sunday urged the people to revive work-from-home, cut down on petrol and diesel consumption, and conserve foreign currency spending due to the pressure from ongoing West Asia conflict.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi framed the appeal as a national duty, stating, “In this time of global crisis, we have to make a resolution keeping duty paramount and fulfil it with complete dedication.”

He also invoked the Covid-19 era measures on working from home and moving to online conferences in national interest, due to supply-chain squeeze. He also advised people to reduce buying gold to reduce the use of foreign exchange and to cut down on international travel.

Another fall-out of the crude-linked inflation and rising logistics and packing costs is a likely price-hike by FMCG companies. This would be on top of the average 3 to 4 percent hike in prices of food, beverage, personal care, and household products which have come into effect.

This is a sign to rediscover the art of making your own bread and brewing your own cuppa at home.

🎱Perplexity’s 24x7 personal assistant is here

Perplexity’s latest offering is a cloud-based, agentic AI system, designed to operate with minimal human oversight. Called the Perplexity Computer, it runs on large language models, enabling it to handle complex tasks autonomously. The orchestration layer deploys more than 20 AI models continuously, functioning as a personal assistant that manages tools, tasks and files across devices and locations.

💡Law firm Trilegal’s entrepreneurial take on the business of law

In a field marked by family-run law firms, Trilegal is an outlier. The law firm set up in 2000 follows an entrepreneurial mindset, with all partners in the firm being awarded equity from 2010 onwards. Its ‘lockstep model’ for awarding equity has created a strong base of 150 entrepreneur-partners over the years.

💊Who is Claire Mazumdar, the successor to Biocon’s legacy

The recent announcement on Biocon’s five-year succession plan by Chairman and Managing Director Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw has the industry created waves. The heir-apparent is Claire Mazumdar, niece of Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw who has been preparing for the role over the years.

➤Claire Mazumdar is the founding chief executive of Bicara Therapeutics, an oncology focused biotech firm, which was listed in 2024 on Nasdaq.

➤Bicara has a manufacturing agreement with Biocon’s subsidiary Syngene.

➤At present, Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw holds 29.92 percent stake in Biocon and her family members are a part of the board.

💲Dev Khare of Lightspeed India on the changing tides in Indian VC ecosystem

Venture Capital investing in India has changed over the years with global firms investing in the market forming deep integrations with the ecosystem here, like Lightspeed. Partner at Lightspeed India Dev Khare believes that the demographic of the Indian entrepreneurs has changed too.

➤Today nearly 80 percent entrepreneurs in Lightspeed’s India portfolio is tech-industry executives or second-time founders.

➤AI has made it easier to start a company, with the potential to create lean startups with fewer people.

➤Upstream of AI startups have a better chance of surviving the AI-bubble, when the cost of intelligence tends to zero.

🚙Tyre-maker CEAT anticipates demand to shrink due to the ongoing war

India’s third-largest tyre maker, CEAT, is worried about softening demand due to the ongoing West Asia conflict. Stressors like rising raw material costs and price hikes have led to an increase in price, says Arnab Banerjee, Managing Director and CEO of CEAT.

➤CEAT is offsetting the loss by targeting new markets like Europe, US and Latin America.

➤The company has already raised prices by 2 to 3 percent since March to counter rise in raw material costs.

➤It is also focusing on making tyres for EV vehicles and premiumisation as an avenue for growth.
