Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

The week was one of recalibration as India’s Wholesale Price Inflation (WPI) reached a 42-month high in April 2026 at 8.3 percent, due to the ongoing West Asia crisis.

The price hike in mineral oil, crude oil and natural gas, basic metals, and other manufacturing and non-food articles caused WPI to more than double from 3.88 in March, said the Ministry of Commerce and Industry. Notably, food inflation remained low.

WPI measures the price of goods traded in bulk at the wholesale or producer level. The inflation in price paid by consumers or retail inflation is measured through the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

Experts believe that the WPI could soon start reflecting at the retail level, impacting consumers. The increase in WPI is already being felt by the manufacturing sector, which will likely pass on the prices to the consumers

The hike in fuel price announced on Friday – Rs 3 per litre for petrol and diesel, and Rs 2 for CNG – is likely to push India’s retail inflation by 25-30 basis points in May. Retail inflation has been relatively stable due to government policies and rose from 3.4 in March to 3.48 percent in April, while food inflation rose from 3.87 in March to 4.2 percent in April.

However, analytics company Crisil predicts that retail inflation is expected to rise to 5.1 percent in the current fiscal (FY27) as the cost is passed on to the consumers eventually.

💊New drug to target progression of Alzheimer's

US-based drugmaker Eli Lilly has launched the intravenous drug Lormalzi (donanemab) in India, used to treat the progression of Alzheimer’s disease. Directed at patients in early stages of the disease, it checks progression, helping preserve memory, thinking ability, and daily functioning in the progressive neurodegenerative disease. However, the drug is steeply priced at Rs 91,688 per 350 grams at present.

👄Clove Dental’s bet on standardising experience

From a fragmented market with no significant chain / brand, Clove Dental has emerged as India’s largest organised dental care chain. Founder Amarinder Singh believes that the business got the basics right – building the trust between patient and practitioner rather than capitalising on the brand.

₹ Rupee on a slippery slope

The Indian Rupee reached an all-time low of 96.14 against the US dollar on Friday, settling at 95.81. The currency has depreciated 11 percent over the past year with 4.7 percent decline since the West Asia conflict started in February. The currency has been under stress due to surging crude oil prices and foreign fund outflows.

➤The currency is likely to continue depreciating unless geopolitical risks ease and crude prices are under control.

➤This has led to intense pressure on India’s current account deficit, balance of payment, forex reserves and overall fiscal health.

➤Experts believe that the government will have to take “harsh” measures to manage immediate pressure on current account deficit and balance of payments.

🚗Tata Motors stalls due to JLR troubles

The headwinds at Tata Motors’ British luxury unit JLR has resulted in a sharp decline in its consolidated FY26 financials, overshadowing its growth in the Indian market. However, the momentum from GST cuts has continued till May and hasn’t been impacted by the West Asia crisis.

➤Tata Motors saw improvement in profitability supported by operating leverage from higher volumes, favourable product mix, structural cost reduction and approval of PLI.

➤Its JLR unit has had to withstand a cyberattack incident disrupting production, higher US tariffs, luxury tax pressure from China, increased marketing expenses, and rising commodity costs.

➤Tata Motors overall market share rose to 14.2 percent in Q4 FY26, making it the second-largest Indian passenger vehicle maker in second half of FY26.
