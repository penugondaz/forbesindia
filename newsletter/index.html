Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

Finally, there will be a number to it – after missing the timeline for the decennial census in 2021, 30 lakh officials in India will spend April 2026 to February 2027 counting the number of people and profiling them as part of its 16th census.

As per public data sources, India’s population surpassed that of China in 2023, and the current estimated population of the country is 1.4 billion making it the most populous country in the world. The exercise which will cost the government an estimated Rs 11,718.24 crore will deliver share data with customised visualisation tools at the village and ward level.

The current census which began on April1 will also collect caste data electronically in the second phase of population enumeration to capture the social and demographic diversity in the country.

The data, if made available, will be significant in recent times as the data from Socio-Economic and Caste Census 2011 was never made public. In a country where elections, benefit schemes and access are closely tied to caste profiles, the data from census 2027 has the power to reshape policy and narratives.

💉Weight loss drugs dodge regulation

The fall in prices of semaglutide-based weight loss drugs in India due to patent expiry is having an adverse impact – the medicines are now accessible to people without prescription.

➤Easy availability and lowered prices have also led to people “trying out” the drugs as a quick fix to weight loss.

➤Despite being classified as Schedule H drugs, pharmacists who are only too happy to sell the drugs without prescription across major Indian cities.

➤In recent weeks, regulators have conducted inspections across retail pharmacies, wholesalers, online pharmacies, and wellness clinics for misuse of the drug.

🏏Bidding for the IPL champions

Times Group’s entry into the business of cricket has been in the making for a few years now. Starting with buying the digital rights for IPL in 2011, it has come a full circle as a consortium of four parties – Aditya Birla Group, Times of India Group, Bolt Ventures and private equity firm Blackstone --acquired majority stake in Royal Challengers Bengaluru. Vice Chairman of Times Internet Satyan Gajwani shared the details.

➤The Times Group was evaluating a stake in Rajasthan Royals versus Royal Challengers Bengaluru.

➤The four-party consortium had been in conversation for a tie-up and firmed it up ahead of the $1.78 billion deal for a stake in RCB and Women’s Premier League team.

➤Gajwani says that the largest driver of growth in IPL will come from the media rights cycle.
