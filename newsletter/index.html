Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

The countdown officially begins today for all five assemblies which went to polls recently – West Bengal, Tamil Nadu, Puducherry, Kerala and Assam.

The exit polls so far point at major upsets for the ruling parties in Bengal and Kerala, and some even talking about a bumper debut for actor-turned politician Vijay-led Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK), though ruling DMK is likely to corner the majority.

Analysis and predictions aside, high voter turnout for the legislative assemblies of Tamil Nadu and West Bengal have set new records since India’s independence. What the voters decide will play out over the week.

The end of the polls also sparked a rumour on potential fuel price-hikes which was later debunked by the Press Information Bureau. However, the recent monthly economic review released by the Finance Ministry said that the rise in oil prices due to the disturbance in West Asia is feeding into inflation directly and indirectly. The report said that the most immediate impact has been visible in the rise in domestic fuel prices, though there is a larger impact on high transport and input costs across commodities and services.

This and the supply chain shock are likely to lead to demand compression over time. It is not over until the war is.

⛽Waste-to-energy startups shine due to rising crude prices

It has been a slow process, but startups focusing on generating biofuels from waste have now come own. With an initial push from SATAT initiative target set by the government to produce 15 million metric tons of CBG annually by 2030, these companies are also riding the tailwinds of rising LPG prices due to the conflict in West Asia. Startups like Carbon Masters, Ecoil, GPS Renewables and Farmwatt, which tackle different aspects of producing biofuels, believe that the current energy squeeze will act as an inflection point in their journey of what they set out to build.

💳How co-branded cards are key to driving repeat use and brand loyalty

India’s co-branded credit card boom is indicative of changing customer preference for seamless payments and personalisation. The segment now makes up nearly 17 percent of 111 million credit cards in India. For the banks and card issuers, this is a channel for new customer acquisition while for the consumer brands, it creates a deeper customer engagement, driving repeat transactions.

🚘Luxury car maker Mercedes to bring plug-in hybrids to India

India’s largest luxury car-maker Mercedes-Benz will launch its first plug-in hybrid in the country in December. The launch of the transitional technology in India will be Mercedes’ step towards gauging consumer demand in India, said MD and CEO Santosh Iyer.

➤The plug-in hybrid will debut in the S-class, priced around Rs 2.2 to Rs 2.3 crore.

➤Mercedes will import the model as a completely built unit and might consider localising it based on demand.

➤Mercedes is pursuing a multi-powertrain strategy in India expanding its electric line-up, while retaining its presence across hybrids and ICE models.

💊Shifting the game from volume to value: GV Prasad, Dr Reddy’s pharma

Dr Reddy’s is in the news as one of the generic drug makers who have benefited from the patent expiry of semaglutides. While this is an important category for the Indian drug maker, the key lies in increasing value in new markets through differentiation, says GV Prasad, Co-chairman and MD of Dr Reddy’s.

➤Dr Reddy’s started early in the peptide-led weight loss category with the launch of liraglutide in Europe.

➤The pharma company would like to be an ecosystem player in the weight loss segment with presence across nutrition and protein supplementation.

➤The company’s M&A focus has largely been on acquiring branded franchises across emerging market brands, Indian brands and OTC consumer businesses.
