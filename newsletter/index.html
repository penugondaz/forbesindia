Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

On Thursday, the Memorandum of Understanding signed by the US President Donald Trump and Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian in Versailles, France on the sidelines of the G7 summit offered a brief spell of relief.Till the world realised that this wasn’t going to be another historical moment, stopping a war which has dragged on for too long, impacting supply chains, oil prices, global energy crisis, and much more.

After Israel continued to bomb Lebanon, Iran claimed to have closed the Strait of Hormuz again on Saturday due to ceasefire violations.

The first round of truce talks has now moved to the Swiss town of Burgenstock. The Iranian delegation is being led by Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, Parliament Speaker and Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi, while the US delegation is led by Vice President JD Vance. The talks are being mediated by the leaders of Pakistan and Qatar.

However, President Trump's comments on Truth Social late on Sunday threatening to bomb Iran 'harder' if they do not scale back the trouble in Lebanon can potentially throw a spanner in the process.

With speculations on a final MoU being signed by Friday, one can hope that Lake Lucerne will work its magic to create history.

💪CEA says India continues to be resilient despite rising oil prices, AI ban

Chief Economic Advisor V Anantha Nageswaran says that India economy is in a bright spot and can get back to February levels once US and Iran call truce on the ongoing war. Apart from the conflict and the pressure on oil prices, India is preparing to face monsoon deficiency, with learnings from the past.

➤Nearly $80 billion is expected to flow into the country from the diaspora and other investors following RBI’s recent measures to encourage forex inflow.

➤The impact of the monsoon deficit on Rabi crops will be watched closely, while Kharif sowing, complimented by water and fertiliser availability, has been on track.

➤In the face of trade-related restrictions, India should think hard and on a sustainable basis about manufacturing, to prepare for the coming decades.

🌿How India is preparing for the El Niño year

India is facing the worst delay in monsoon of the decade, while the world over, temperature and rainfall patterns will face major disruptions from June onwards. The phenomenon also brings with it the threat of spikes in retail food prices and headline inflation.

➤Due to the El Niño effect, India’s inflation for FY27 is forecast to be between 5.2 to 5.6 percent.

➤The impact of the phenomenon on farm sector’s contribution to the economy, rural income, and aggregate demand has weakened in recent years due to structural shifts in rural India, including higher irrigated area and crop diversification.

➤The Rabi crop output is expected to be 3.2 percent higher than last year.

🔇The ban on Fable 5 model is pushing Indian tech cos to rethink strategy

The export control directive by the US government banning the launch of frontier AI company Anthropic’s Mythos 5 and Fable 5 models has ushered in a new era of geopolitical restrictions on access to technology. The ban has impacted companies across the spectrum, while raising pertinent questions on cybersecurity.

➤The ban pushes the case for the adoption of AI model-agnostic architecture by enterprises as well as others to hedge the risk of shutdowns.

➤The ban also challenges the way software can be susceptible to bans like the one imposed by the US.

➤Self-sufficient technologies will be critical in isolating Indian companies from hostile states and actors.
