Hello and welcome back to Forbes India Weekly Digest!

The reigning champions Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) flagged the IPL season in style, beating Sunrisers Hyderabad by six wickets at the M Chinnaswamy Stadium.

The security was tight and the administration cautious after the stampede of last year claiming 11 lives during RCB’s victory parade.

While all eyes are on RCB for an encore, the team also has an interesting mix backing it – a consortium of Aditya Birla Group, The Times of India Group, Bolt Ventures and Blackstone’s perpetual private equity strategy BXPE. The consortium has also acquired 100 percent of the Women’s Premiere League franchise in addition to RCB from Diageo subsidiary, United Spirits Limited.

The $1.78 billion transaction was paid upfront as per the new BCCI rules, instead of a period of 10 years. Blackstone is treading on familiar grounds, as PE firms worldwide have been known to invest in sports franchises. Closer home, CVC Capital Partners acquired 67 percent stake in Gujarat Titans in 2021, exiting partly in 2025.

The transaction marks Blackstone’s maiden investment in sports and an exposure to the nearly $10 billion IPL economy. On a sidenote, Kohli memes have already started flooding social media!

💵Abhimanyu Munjal: securing Hero Fincorp’s future

🌆 Maharashtra’s economy set to cross UAE and Singapore, says Fadnavis

Maharashtra has global ambitions, as was evident from Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis’s vision for the state. The state’s economy which is pegged at $660 billion at present is set to surpass that of UAE and Singapore in the next two years.

➤The CM highlighted that the power-surplus state was a major draw for data centres with nine gigawatts of new capacity in the pipeline.

➤Maharashtra is planning for sustainable growth with 52 percent contribution for power from renewable energy sources by 2030.

➤The upcoming Vadhavan port will rank among the top 10 globally in terms of container traffic.

🚘Audi is building a sustainable India portfolio

Currency volatility, global unrest and rising prices are likely to dampen the growth for luxury car markets this year. German luxury auto-maker Audi is bracing for a modest expansion in keeping with the trend, says India head Balbir Singh Dhillon.

➤After a robust performance in 2023, the brand faced declining sales and recorded two percent growth last year.

➤While the brand chose petrol and EV, exiting the diesel vehicle segment, the pace of growth for EVs has been subdued.

➤In a market driven by luxury car repurchase, Audi is guarding its spot by spreading across entry and high-end models, while building a pre-owned ecosystem.

🎨Luxor brings Crayola’s creativity brand to India

This is a call for all adults (and kids) to bring out their colouring books as Luxor has signed a long-term agreement with Crayola to bring its products to the Indian market. The deal includes Crayola manufacturing its products in India, feeding into its supply-chain.

➤As part of the agreement, the brand’s products will be marketed as “Luxor-Crayola” and will be priced at a premium to maintain quality.

➤India’s stationery market is estimated to be Rs 12,000 crore, with Camlin dominating children’s stationery.

➤The creativity and colouring segment are growing at15-20 percent annually, a higher rate of growth compared to the overall stationery segment.
