The adage “one man’s loss is another man’s gain” played out real-time this week as Anthropic’s refusal to drop safeguards to work with the US Department of War (DoW - previously US Department of Defense) resulted in OpenAI scooping up the deal.

In a discussion this week with Pentagon officials, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei said that the use of its AI tools to make final military targeting decision in case of fully autonomous weapons without proper oversight, and use of Anthorpic’s tools for mass domestic surveillance are red lines which the company was not willing to toe.

On Friday, the US administration asked all agencies under it to stop using Anthropic and imposed penalties on the firm on Friday. According to the Wall Street Journal, Anthropic’s deal with the Pentagon is estimated to be worth $200 million.

By Saturday, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman said that the company had reached an agreement with the DoW to deploy its model in the Pentagon’s classified network.

While Anthropic said that it will contest the decision, Pentagon is also likely to have similar discussions with Alphabet and xAI to add them to its classifieds network, according to media reports.

This isn’t the end of the matter as we know it and will set a global precedent for similar moves.

Will track every traffic offence, cancel licences of repeat offenders: Gadkari

You might soon have to deal with the graded penalty points system for driving license as part of government’s push to crackdown on reckless driving. Minister for Road Transport and Highways Nitin Gadkari said that the proposed system will be a step towards introducing consequences in the country with “no fear or respect for the law.”

🔹The proposed graded penalty points system will assign points for traffic violations with repeat offenders risking suspension and cancellation of license.

🔹Nearly 1.8 lakh people are killed annually in India due to reckless driving, costing the country 3 percent of the GDP.

🔹In order to make roads safer, the government is looking at a four-prong strategy including road engineering, vehicle engineering, advance safety systems and stricter law enforcement.

📈GDP growth forecast revised to 7-7.4 percent

India’s GDP growth forecast for FY27 has been revised to 7-7.4 percent from the earlier projection of 6.8-7.2 percent made in January as part of the Economic Survey. The projection has received a fillip from strong economic momentum, landmark trade framework with the US, and robust high-frequency indicators across key sectors.

🔹Chief Economic Advisor V Anantha Nageswaran however cautioned against global uncertainties as an external risk factor.

🔹The CEA highlighted a notable pickup in rural consumption which had been muted so far.

🔹He added that India’s limited exposure to AI portfolios in capital markets could prove to be a tailwind for capital inflow as global investors reassess AI-heavy portfolios.

🚦D2C brands prefer caution to speed

With mushrooming D2C brands across categories, their exit to FMCG giants has picked pace in recent times. However, turning in profits as part of a structured corporate system has multiple points of friction which can be countered by the brands themselves by focusing on key metrics.

🔹India has close to 800 active D2C brands today across the segments of beauty, personal care, fashion, food, home and electronics.

🔹Indian D2C market is expected to grow at 25-30 percent annually from $12-15 billion in 2025.

🔹Only brands combining gross margins, repeat purchase rates over 40 percent and sustainable EBITDA will endure.
