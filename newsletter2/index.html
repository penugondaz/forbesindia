Graphic of the Week GCC remittance share slides

The GCC’s dominance in India's inward remittances is facing a structural cooldown. Data reveals a near 9-point slide, with the region's share tumbling from 46.7 percent in 2016-17 to 37.9 percent in 2023-24. While the UAE maintains its status as a critical corridor at 19.2 percent, the broader decline across heavyweights like Saudi Arabia and Kuwait signals a significant migration pivot. The Indian diaspora is increasingly moving away from traditional labour roles in the Gulf toward high-skill, high-value positions in Western economies like the US, UK, and Canada, as well as tech hubs like Singapore.
