The baby in the boom and the drought

Dear reader,

So, Andhra Pradesh had to undo in 2024 what it had imposed in 1994—three decades of barring candidates with more than two children from contesting local body elections.

And since the past two years, the state’s Chief Minister N Chandrababu Naidu has been urging couples to have more kids, even going so far as to incentivise them with up to Rs 40,000 to do so, the kind of measures more commonly associated with ageing economies like Japan or South Korea.

The change in the state here is stark. When Andhra Pradesh introduced the two-child norm, its fertility rate stood at around 3. By 2024, it had fallen below 2.

And it isn’t just Andhra. While it has taken India decades to bring down its fertility levels, states like Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka and Delhi got there first. But as the country settles in its low-fertility era, the question now arises: Will India get old before it gets rich?

And of course we covered this reversal in fertility trends this week!

But for those among you who followed Zepto’s IPO coverage instead, our lead graphic puts the numbers in perspective. Daily orders jumped 70 percent between Q4FY25 and Q4FY26. Impressive, isn’t it? That is until you see that category leader Blinkit grew faster, nearly doubling to 3 million daily orders, widening the gap even as Zepto makes its public market debut.

But what of the startups that are already listed? After six years of deep losses, 42 listed startups swung to combined profitability in FY25, led by Paytm’s dramatic turnaround.

And as India’s capital inflows buckled through 2025-26, investor confidence in India’s pet sector touched a high despite fewer startups.

Also check out our conversation with JSW scion Parth Jindal as he makes the case for professionalising the conglomerate and ensuring it isn’t built around one family’s shoulders alone. And yes, don’t forget to watch us discuss India’s youngest billionaires.

Until next time!

-Samreen
