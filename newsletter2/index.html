Only Indian aboard Air Force One

Dear Reader,

By now, you must have heard the news. Sanjay Mehrotra, the Indian origin chairman, president and CEO of Micron Technology, has just became a billionaire.

And no, this newsletter isn’t going to be about how this BITS-Pilani alumnus was thrice denied a visa to the US about five decades ago. It isn’t even going to be about how he still graduated from the University of California, Berkeley and set up SanDisk where he remained until it was acquired in 2016. And it most certainly isn’t going to be about how he was the only Indian-origin business leader to accompany US President Donald Trump on his visit to Beijing earlier this month, which included the likes of Jensen Huang, Tim Cook and Elon Musk among a dozen others.

This is about what comes next. Now that an Indian-origin businessman has made it to the chip billionaire’s table, the real question is, when does an Indian semiconductor company pull off a similar trick?

So, this week we take a look at how the semiconductor boom has minted several billionaires led by Nvidia’s Jensen Huang with a net worth of $183.7 billion. Amidst this global AI surge, India has officially surpassed the US in AI model training startups. And as one of India’s oldest conglomerates met for an unusual board meeting this week, dive into our data story to understand how the Tata Group faces a massive seven-fold spike in losses across its unlisted companies driven by Air India and Tata Digital. On the economic front, a new UN report shows how India’s affordable housing crisis worsened with a 29 million housing unit shortage, while the informal sector hit a record 15 crore jobs despite a decline in hired workers.

And was ‘Tanha Dil’ ever on loop on your playlist? Well, 25 years after the song became a sensation (would you believe it! It’s been 25 years!) singer Shaan sits down with Forbes India to discuss everything from Indie pop to AI.

Also catch actors Tamannaah Bhatia and Bhumi Pednekar as they discuss navigating survival and success as self-made women in Bollywood at Forbes India’s W-Power 2026 awards night.

Until next time!

-Samreen

Micron CEO Sanjay Mehrotra has officially joined the ranks of semiconductor billionaires with a net worth of $1.2 billion, according to real time wealth tracking by Forbes. Other major industry figures on the list include Broadcom’s Henry Samueli ($39.6 billion), Chinese chipmaker Cambricon’s Chen Tianshi (36.7 billion) and Taiwanese semiconductor giant TSMC’s Morris Chang ($9.6 billion).
