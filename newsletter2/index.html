Is raising capital also rocket science?

Dear Reader,

When I caught up with Pawan Chandana last November for a Forbes India space tech feature, the Skyroot CEO told me that raising capital was harder than building a rocket. In fact, his exact words were, “I always say building a rocket is rocket science, but raising capital was even bigger rocket science.” It is hardly a surprise then that the startup’s first Rs100 crore came from entrepreneurs, not venture capitalists, precisely because founders carry a far higher risk appetite.

Until GIC led their Series B in October 2023, half of Chandana’s time was consumed by fundraising. And even after hours of negotiations, barely 10 investors said yes; hundreds turned them down.

And look how time flips fates. Skyroot has just become the first space tech startup in India to attain unicorn status—and is set to add another feather to its cap within weeks, as it prepares to become the first private Indian company to launch its rocket Vikram-I into space.

Elsewhere, Earth-observation startup GalaxEye is targeting a 20-satellite constellation by 2029. So, does all this signal that India’s private space tech sector is finally having its coming-of-age moment? Read our reportage to find out.

From space to our plates, this week we compare food inflation across countries. India’s food inflation stood at 4.2 percent in April, ranking third highest among major economies, trailing only Russia and Brazil. On the crime front, Jaipur recorded a financial crime rate five times the national city average.

And as the government is pushing Indians away from physical gold, check our video report on how history shows that raising duties can fuel smuggling, and sovereign gold bond redemptions could create new financial challenges.

Until next time!
-Samreen
