The Film Star and Her Ford

Dear Reader,

A home away from home. That’s what Bill Ford, Ford’s executive chairman, had to say about Chennai in 2016, almost two decades after the automobile manufacturer set up its first factory in the region. That is, besides the United States, of course.

And Chennai, or Madras as it was still known back then, wasn’t even the first choice for Ford to set up the plant. Among the other alternatives Ford had were Pune and Gurgaon since the carmaker felt the opportunity lay in the North and the West.

It all started when Jayalalithaa, Tamil Nadu’s late chief minister, sensed with some intuition what getting Ford to Tamil Nadu would signify especially since India had just opened up its economy. A mixture of an already established auto parts sector, port, manpower, bureaucracy push and Jayalalithaa’s charisma guaranteed that Chennai seized this from Maharashtra.

Hyundai, BMW, Daimler AG, Mitsubishi and Yamaha among many others followed in the region to create a production ecosystem so robust it eventually earned Madras city the moniker “Detroit of Asia”, capable of producing three cars a minute.

This week we track what the rise of another film star in the same state would mean for business. We analyse Vijay’s high-profile electoral debut in Tamil Nadu and its implications for status quo.

Among other things we also look at the high-stakes AI valuation race, where Anthropic is challenging OpenAI’s dominance with a staggering $900 billion target and why India’s judicial backlog, despite the recent proposal to increase the Supreme Court’s strength, remains a critical bottleneck.

And finally, unless you were living under a rock, you must have heard of the Met Gala this week. Check out our wrap-up of Indian icons at Met Gala 2026 and stay for an essential report on the fake paneer crackdown.

Until next time!
-Samreen
