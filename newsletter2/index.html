Nolan Loves the World’s Loudest Camera— Even Though It Needs a Silencer

Dear Reader,

Why isn’t every film an IMAX film? Is the IMAX hype just giant screens and salary-threatening tickets? Does “shot with” versus “filmed for” IMAX actually matter?

Most audiences likely don’t care—though without hard data, that’s just a guess.

The truth though, lies in the clarity. IMAX offers a vibrant, grain-free world but devours 1,000 feet of film every three minutes. While other directors use the format sparingly, Christopher Nolan went all-in for Odyssey, filming entirely on IMAX. The production utilised a staggering two million feet of film stock! I am not making this up, he said so himself.

IMAX cameras are also famously noisy. So, for Odyssey, Nolan debuted a new silencer-equipped version, sparing actors from shouting over the roaring, lawn mower-like machines.

Turns out silencing cameras were the easy part. But silencing competition?

Project Hail Mary seems to be the latest victim of the Dhurandhar storm—a release so explosive that the record-breaking runs of Pushpa 2 and Pathaan now look like warm-up acts. Apparently, the Ryan Gosling-starrer is struggling for IMAX airtime.

From the silver screen to the effects of the Gulf war on the economy, we examine a different kind of disruption this time. Check out our features on the slowdown in India’s growth forecasts and informal hiring, then stay for our exclusives: FILA 2026 CEO of the Year, and Mumbai’s hungriest rapper.

Until next time!

-Samreen
